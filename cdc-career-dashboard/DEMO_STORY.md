# CDC Career Dashboard — Story Demo Script
**University of St. Thomas · Career Development Center**
Total time: ~15 minutes

---

## SETUP

Before the room arrives:
- Backend running: `cd backend && npm start`
- Frontend running: `cd frontend && npm start`
- Browser open at **http://localhost:3000**, logged out
- `demo-events.csv` ready to upload (it's in the root project folder)
- Open this file on a second screen or phone — do not show it to the audience

---

## THE STORY

> Two students. Same university. Same CDC.
> One will graduate with a job offer. One will walk across the stage and disappear.
> The difference isn't talent. It's visibility.

---

## ACT 1 — THE INVISIBLE STUDENT (2 min)

*Start logged out. Don't click anything yet.*

> "I want to tell you about Caleb Robinson.
>
> Caleb is a Year 1 Accounting student. He came to one advising appointment in September. Since then — nothing. No events, no applications, no contact with the CDC. Right now, nobody knows Caleb exists in our pipeline. He's not on anybody's list. He's not sending red flags. He's just... quiet.
>
> The question is: do we find Caleb before it's too late, or do we find out at commencement?"

→ Click **I'm Staff** → enter password → sign in

> "This is what the CDC sees when it opens the dashboard."

Point to the **Need Outreach** card:

> "Nine students like Caleb. Nine students who are not engaging with any of our platforms. Not a vague concern — a specific number, this morning, based on data we already have."

→ Click **Need Outreach** filter

> "There he is."

Point to **Caleb Robinson** in the table — score 10, 0 events, 0 applications.

> "Score of 10. That's one advising appointment from September. That's all we have on Caleb."

---

## ACT 2 — WHAT WE KNOW ABOUT CALEB (1 min)

→ Click **View Journey →** on Caleb Robinson

> "This is Caleb's career roadmap. Year 1 — the Explore phase. Twelve tasks his first year should cover.
>
> How many has he completed? Zero.
>
> PathwayU? Never logged in. LinkedIn? We don't know. Handshake? One appointment. That's the whole picture.
>
> Without this, Caleb is a name on a list. With this, he's a student with a specific gap that we can close today."

→ Hit **Back to Staff Dashboard**

---

## ACT 3 — SOMETHING JUST CHANGED (3 min)

*This is the live upload moment. Pause for effect.*

> "Last Tuesday, we ran the Spring Career Fair. Caleb showed up.
>
> He walked in, talked to three employers, attended a resume workshop afterward. First time we've seen him since September.
>
> Here's what that looks like."

→ Go to **Admin** tab → **Upload Career Data**

> "This is the Handshake export from Tuesday's fair. I'm going to upload it now."

→ Upload `demo-events.csv` → watch the success message

> "Let's go back to the staff dashboard."

→ Click **Staff View**

Point to the summary cards — they now show **6 Need Outreach, 4 Developing**:

> "Three students just moved. Caleb is no longer in Need Outreach.
>
> One event. One workshop. That's the difference between invisible and visible.
>
> The dashboard caught it. We didn't have to go looking."

→ Click **Developing** filter → find Caleb → click **View Journey →**

> "Caleb's score moved from 10 to 50. And look at his Year 1 roadmap — 'Attend introductory CDC workshops and events' just got checked automatically. The system linked the event to the milestone.
>
> He still has 11 tasks to go. But now we know he's moving."

---

## ACT 4 — WHAT SUCCESS LOOKS LIKE (2 min)

→ Back to Staff Dashboard → click **Engaged** filter → click **View Journey →** on **Marcus Johnson**

> "Now let me show you the other end of the story.
>
> Marcus Johnson. Year 4, Business Administration. Score 175. Currently interviewing at UnitedHealth Group.
>
> This is what engagement looks like by senior year."

Point to progress cards:

> "He's completed about 12% of his Year 4 milestones — the ones we can verify automatically from Handshake. The rest he'll self-report as he does them."

Open **Year 4: Implement** → scroll through tasks:

> "Every task in the roadmap is specific. Not 'network more' — 'Use St. Thomas Connect to reconnect with alumni and request coffee chats.' Not 'practice interviews' — 'Use Big Interview for final prep before company meetings.'
>
> Marcus knows exactly what he should be doing. And his advisor knows exactly where he is."

→ Click **Applications** stat card

> "Five applications. UnitedHealth — interviewing. Mayo Clinic — pending. This isn't a guess. It's the actual pipeline from Handshake."

→ Click **Get Recommendations**

> "Even though Marcus is engaged, the system still finds gaps. He hasn't started his negotiation prep — there are CDC workshops on salary negotiation he hasn't attended yet. The recommendation links him directly there.
>
> The system doesn't just track what happened. It points to what's next."

→ Close modal

---

## ACT 5 — THE COHORT PICTURE (2 min)

→ Back to Staff Dashboard → click **Analytics & Insights**

> "Zoom out. This is the whole cohort."

Point to Activity Stats:

> "Events: average 1.1 per student. Median of zero. Half the cohort has never attended a single career event. That's not Caleb's problem — that's a program design question.
>
> Applications: average 2.4. That's better. Students know how to use Handshake for job searching, but not for showing up."

Point to Platform Usage:

> "Applications dominate. Events are second. CDC appointments are a distant third — only 9 total across 16 students. Students are using Handshake to apply, but they're not coming in to see us.
>
> Roadmap self-check: zero. Nobody has used this feature yet — because they haven't been shown it. That's our opportunity this week."

Point to Actionable Insights:

> "The system doesn't just show you the data. It tells you what to do.
>
> 'Six students have never attended a career event — promote upcoming Handshake events and send targeted outreach.'
>
> That's not a dashboard observation. That's a Monday morning task."

---

## ACT 6 — THE STUDENT SIDE (1 min)

→ Log out → **I'm a Student** → type `STU001` → Continue

> "One more perspective. This is what Marcus sees when he logs in.
>
> His own roadmap. His own progress. The recommendations point him to platforms, not generic advice.
>
> He can check tasks as he completes them — attach a screenshot, a letter, a certificate. It stays in his record. His advisor sees it.
>
> There's no overlap with other students. He entered his ID — he sees his profile, nothing else."

---

## CLOSING (1 min)

> "Three things this changes.
>
> **For Caleb:** He went from invisible to visible because of one career fair. We caught it. We have a reason to reach out today instead of in April.
>
> **For Marcus:** He has a clear runway to graduation. No ambiguity about what's left to do.
>
> **For the CDC:** You start every Monday knowing the exact nine students who need a personal email — not a mass newsletter, not a hope that they'll walk in. A name, a number, and a reason.
>
> The data was always there. Now it works for us."

## IF INTERRUPTED WITH A QUESTION

**"How does the score work?"**
> "Events are worth 20 points, applications 15, CDC appointments 10 — all pulled from Handshake exports. It's a proxy for platform engagement, not a grade. The roadmap milestones are the qualitative picture."

**"What if a student lies about completing a task?"**
> "They can attach proof — a screenshot, a PDF, a certificate. It's optional, but it's there. Ultimately the advisor sees the same roadmap and can have a conversation about it."

**"Can advisors edit the tasks?"**
> "Yes — Admin panel, Manage Milestones. Add a task, change the wording, assign an auto-trigger so it gets checked automatically when the right Handshake data comes in."

**"What about students who don't have Handshake?"**
> "The roadmap is still fully usable. They self-report every task manually. The Handshake data just auto-fills the ones we can verify."

**"Is this live data?"**
> "Yes. Every score you see was calculated from Handshake CSVs uploaded to this system. The upload we just did — that was real. Those three students actually moved."
